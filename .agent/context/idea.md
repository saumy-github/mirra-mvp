# Mirra Idea

## Overview

Mirra aims to deliver a full virtual try-on experience for a specific person. To do this, we want to create a measurement-specific digital twin and combine it with clothing that behaves realistically, so the final result shows how a garment may actually look and fit on that person's body rather than on a generic model.

This vision can be understood in three broad steps.

## Step 1: Personal Digital Twin

The first step is to create a unique digital twin for each person. This digital twin should reflect the person's real body measurements as closely as possible and become their persistent body profile inside the system.

What we want from this step:

- A body representation that is specific to one person, not a generic size template
- Strong alignment with real-world measurements so garment fit can be judged meaningfully
- A reusable digital twin that does not need to be rebuilt every time
- A foundation that can improve over time in body accuracy, realism, and human likeness
- Enough structural understanding of the body so clothing can later be placed and simulated correctly

The purpose of this step is not only to show a 3D body, but to establish a dependable base on which all future clothing experiences can happen.

## Step 2: Product Ingestion and 3D Clothing Asset Creation

The second step is to build a clothing pipeline that starts from garment images and product information, then turns them into reusable 3D clothing assets. These assets should carry the essential identity of the garment so they can later be used in try-on experiences across many users.

What we want from this step:

- A system that can take garment images and clothing metadata as inputs
- A way to convert 2D garments into reusable 3D clothing assets
- Clothing assets that preserve real size intent rather than acting like generic visual shells
- Garments that carry their colors, prints, textures, and overall design identity
- Proper garment structure so the clothing can later behave in a believable way
- A reusable asset library that can serve many future try-on sessions

The purpose of this step is to create a strong clothing foundation so garments are not recreated from scratch every time and can be prepared for realistic try-on.

## Step 3: Virtual Try-On Experience

The third step is to combine the person's digital twin with the prepared clothing assets and generate a full virtual try-on result. This is where the body and garment come together so the person can see how a specific piece of clothing appears on their own digital twin.

What we want from this step:

- Clean combination of the avatar and the clothing asset
- Proper placement of garments on the body before and during try-on
- A final result that shows how the garment looks and fits on that specific person
- A believable visual output that feels complete and polished
- A presentation layer where the try-on can be shown clearly to the user
- Final accessibility through a website or app

The purpose of this step is to deliver the actual virtual try-on experience that the user sees and interacts with.

## Long-Term Direction

Together, these three steps define the broader vision of Mirra:

- First, understand the person well enough to build their digital twin
- Then, understand the garment well enough to turn it into a reusable digital clothing asset
- Then, bring both together into a complete virtual try-on experience

Everything else in the system should support these three outcomes: a trustworthy digital body, a reusable clothing asset pipeline, and a believable try-on experience built around both.
