# Discussion Phase

## Overview

The Discussion Phase is the foundation of every development task. This is a collaborative exploration phase where we align on requirements, explore options, and identify potential issues **before** any planning or coding begins.

## Key Principle

**No files or code are generated in this phase.** This is purely conversational and exploratory.

## What Happens

### 1. Understanding the Task

- You describe what you want to accomplish
- I ask clarifying questions to fully understand the requirements
- We discuss the context and goals

### 2. Exploration & Options

- I suggest alternative approaches if applicable
- We evaluate different solutions and their trade-offs
- I point out potential better options based on:
  - Best practices
  - Existing codebase patterns
  - Performance considerations
  - Maintainability

### 3. Risk & Vulnerability Assessment

- I identify potential vulnerabilities or risks
- We discuss edge cases and failure scenarios
- I flag any breaking changes or compatibility issues
- We consider security implications

### 4. Clarification & Refinement

- You ask me questions about the approach
- I ask you questions to clarify ambiguities
- We iterate until we have a shared understanding

## When This Phase Ends

This phase concludes when:

- ✅ We both understand what needs to be done
- ✅ All major questions have been answered
- ✅ Potential risks and vulnerabilities are identified
- ✅ We agree on the general approach
- ✅ You explicitly ask to move to the Planning Phase

## Output

**No files or code.** Only a shared understanding documented in our conversation.

## Next Phase

Once discussion is complete, we move to the [Planning Phase](02-planning-phase.md) where I'll create detailed plan files.
